# SIREN v0.2 — Product Guidelines

## Prose Style
- **Tone:** Direct and Actionable. Communication should be clean, straightforward, and easy to understand, especially during high-stress situations. Avoid technical jargon when direct action is required.
- **Guideline:** Logs and documentation should prioritize immediate utility and forensic accuracy.

## Branding & Aesthetic
- **Visual Identity:** Tactical/Command-Center. The brand should evoke an aggressive, high-intensity, and authoritative presence, suitable for both forensic investigation and home defense.
- **Guideline:** Use bold typography, high-contrast color schemes, and layouts that resemble data-rich command centers.

## UX Principles
- **Core Principle:** Reactive/Urgent Access. Prioritize fast reaction times and immediate access to denial modes. The user should be able to toggle interference and switch modes with minimal cognitive load.
- **Guideline:** Ensure critical controls (e.g., Denial Toggle, Master Gain, Mode Switch) are always within reach and clearly identifiable.

## Feedback & Alerts
- **System Communication:** Use a multi-layered approach for critical events:
    - **Non-Intrusive/Passive:** For standard operations, use subtle visual cues and detailed background logging for later forensic analysis.
    - **High-Contrast/Active Alerts:** For critical detections or system failures, use high-intensity visual and audio indicators to command immediate attention.
    - **Guided/Soothing Feedback:** For home defense and stress/pain relief scenarios, provide step-by-step guidance and auditory feedback that is calming yet effective.
